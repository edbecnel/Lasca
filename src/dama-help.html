Dama - Help & Rules

This app supports two Dama variants on an 8×8 board: Standard and International.

Variants (Standard vs International)

Standard (Immediate capture removal)

  • When you jump an opponent piece, it is removed immediately.

International (End-of-sequence capture removal)

  • Jumped pieces are captured but remain on the board until your capture sequence ends.
  • Captured-but-not-yet-removed pieces are marked as pending removal during the sequence.
  • You cannot jump the same captured piece twice in the same turn.

You can switch between variants using the game’s variant selector.

Save File Compatibility: Save files from Dama Classic Standard and Dama Classic International are interoperable. You can load a save from either variant into the other, and it will automatically adapt to the current variant's capture removal rules. When you save again, it will use the currently running variant.

Game Setup

  • Board: 8×8
  • 12 pieces per side
  • White moves first

Game Rules

Movement

  • Men: move diagonally forward one square.
  • Officers (Kings): move diagonally any distance in any direction ("flying" king for both captures and quiet moves).

Promotion

  • A man promotes to an officer upon reaching the far edge.
  • Promotion occurs at the end of a capture sequence (no mid-sequence king powers). If a man reaches the far edge at any point during a capture chain, it promotes after the chain ends (even if it does not finish on the far edge).

Captures & Multi-Captures

Captures are mandatory when available. If you can continue capturing with the same piece, you must continue until no further captures are available.

How captures work

  • Men: capture by jumping diagonally over an adjacent enemy to the empty square beyond (forward or backward).
  • Officers: capture by jumping diagonally over exactly one enemy piece and landing on any empty square beyond it.
  • Officer zigzag rule (multi-captures): during a capture chain, an Officer cannot make the next capture along the same diagonal direction as the previous capture, and cannot reverse direction either. In practice, Officers must alternate between the two diagonal directions (a “zigzag”).

Choosing among captures

  • When multiple capture lines exist, the game enforces the maximum-capture rule: you must choose a capture that can result in the greatest total number of captured pieces.
International note: In end-of-sequence capture removal, jumped pieces stay visible until the sequence ends. The marker indicates they are already captured and cannot be jumped again.

Using the Interface

  • Click a piece to select it.
  • Legal destinations appear as highlighted circles.
  • Move History: Click any entry (including "Start") to jump to that point in the game. Undo/Redo also navigates through history, and the Move History list scrolls to keep the current entry visible.
  • In Dama International (end-of-sequence capture removal), captured-but-not-yet-removed pieces are marked with red circles to show they are pending removal.
  • During a capture chain, you must continue with the same piece until the chain ends.
  • AI controls can be paused/resumed, and you can use Step to advance a single AI move when it is an AI-controlled side’s turn.
  • Mobile: On touch devices, the ↕️ button near the board toggles reduced board height (useful if the bottom navigation bar overlaps the board). You can touch-hold + drag to reposition it.
